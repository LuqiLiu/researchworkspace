# Research Workspace Lite V1.2

V1.2 adds a deliberately small team knowledge library. It provides a common
place for useful research material without introducing social-media mechanics
or weakening the existing private-content boundary.

## Team knowledge library

- Add `/community/` as the shared knowledge homepage for active accounts.
- Browse team-shared records newest first and filter by keyword, record type,
  project, author, or tag.
- Search titles, normalized body text, tags, project names, and comments.
- Keep team-only records out of the existing **分享给我的** view, which remains
  an overview of explicit direct and project access.
- Include team access in the owner's **我发出的分享** roster so the owner can
  see every active account that currently has access.

## Permission model

- Any active signed-in account can view and comment on a team-shared record.
- Team sharing alone never grants edit or share-management permission.
- The owner and existing explicitly authorized editors retain their higher
  permissions.
- Deactivated accounts do not receive team access.
- Private and archived records never appear in the team library.
- Public profile snapshots remain a separate opt-in publication mechanism and
  are not changed by team sharing.

## Attachment boundary

Team visibility applies to the Markdown body only by default. The owner must
separately enable team attachment access. Disabling body sharing immediately
clears the team attachment flag, and protected download and inline-image routes
continue to enforce the effective attachment permission.

## New-record defaults

- `PAPER`, `WRITING`, and `RESOURCE` start with team sharing selected.
- `NOTE`, `IDEA`, `EXPERIMENT`, and `ISSUE` start private.
- The choice is always visible and editable before the first save.
- Paper imports follow the paper default, while imported PDFs remain excluded
  unless attachment sharing is separately selected.

## Upgrade safety

Apply migration `research_objects.0005_team_visibility`. It adds
`is_shared_with_team` and `share_team_attachments`, both with a database default
of `false`. Consequently, every record created before V1.2 remains private from
the team after migration. No historical content is automatically published or
shared.

V1.2 intentionally has no likes, follows, popularity rankings, leaderboards,
engagement counters, or recommendation algorithms.
